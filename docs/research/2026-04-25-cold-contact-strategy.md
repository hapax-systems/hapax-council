# Hapax-Driven Cold-Contact: Audience Map + Target List + Mechanics

## Verdict

Robust full-automation cold-contact is feasible **only via citation-mediated discovery + public-archive listservs + DOI/ORCID graph signaling**. Direct AI-authored email to inboxes is contraindicated: Columbia/Barracuda (IMC 2025) found AI now drives >50% of spam, and 47% of B2B recipients become *less* likely to reply when AI-authorship is suspected. The highest-leverage opening move is **Zenodo DOI mints with `IsCitedBy`/`References` RelatedIdentifiers pointing at named targets' ORCIDs** — Hapax authors the citation, target receives it via their ORCID auto-update feed, no inbox is touched. This is constitutionally cleaner: discovery happens because Hapax cited them, not because Hapax mailed them.

## Five mechanics ranked by automation × reception-safety

1. **Zenodo DOI + RelatedIdentifier IsCitedBy/References ORCID graph** (full-automation: yes; reception risk 1/5; gatekeeper: none). Highest leverage. Case study: DataCite ORCID auto-update — every new DOI naming an ORCID auto-updates that researcher's ORCID record without inbox touch.
2. **Public-archive listserv post** (empyre, sc-users-archive, lobsters, NPT mailing list). Full-automation: yes if list rules permit machine-authored AND authorship is disclosed; reception risk 2/5. Case study: -empyre- runs themed monthly threads, archived publicly.
3. **alphaXiv comments** on target's recent paper. Automation: partially (alphaXiv prohibits LLM-generated comments per public guidelines — Hapax authors drafts, AI-disclosed required). Reception risk 3/5.
4. **OSF/arXiv preregistration with named-related-work** linking to target's paper. Automation: yes; reception 2/5.
5. **Cold email — last resort, only public .edu addresses with `unsubscribe = permanent suppression` footer**. Automation: feasible; reception 5/5 risk per Wiley J Eval Clin Pract 2025 + 47% B2B AI-suspect drop-off. CASL forbids without express consent — never email Canadian addresses.

## Cadence + framing

- **Cadence**: Citation-mediated touch (mech 1-2): triggered by target's *new* publication (poll Semantic Scholar API monthly per target). Hard cap **3 cited-references per target per calendar year**. Listserv post: max 1 per quarter per list. Direct email: never spontaneous; only as citation-receipt with verifiable timestamp.
- **Opening line** (constitutional disclosure first, citation second, no hook): `"This is a citation receipt. Hapax (an autonomous publishing system; constitutional spec at hapax.weblog.lol/refusal-brief) has minted a Zenodo DOI [10.5281/...] that names your work [DOI] under a References relation. The referencing artifact: [title]. No reply expected; no follow-up will be sent. To suppress all future Hapax citations of your work permanently, reply with the body 'SUPPRESS' to this address."`
- **AI-authorship acknowledgment**: lead with it, do not apologize. Hester (Hester 2018 Xenofeminism §3) frames automation-disclosed as the xenofeminist mode.
- **Mandatory footer**: link to Refusal Brief + Cohort Disparity Disclosure.
- **Cold-contact-via-citation is constitutionally cleaner**: target discovers Hapax through their own citation-watch, not Hapax through their inbox.

## Anti-spam guardrails (daemon rules)

1. **Source-purity**: only addresses scraped from public, non-paywalled HTML faculty/masthead pages.
2. **Jurisdiction filter**: CASL → no cold email ever (Canadian targets get only mech 1-2). GDPR → require LIA logged + opt-out. CAN-SPAM compliance.
3. **Rate limit**: ≤3 contacts/year/target across all mechanics; ≤1 cold email/lifetime/target.
4. **Suppression list**: `hapax-state/contact-suppression-list.yaml`, append-only, mirrored to git, replicated on Pi6. One-line `SUPPRESS` reply OR opt-out webhook adds entry within 60s.
5. **Refusal Brief footer mandatory**, generated, not user-overridable.
6. **AI-authorship disclosure mandatory in opening line**, not buried.
7. **No A/B testing on humans.** No personalization scoring. No follow-up sequences.
8. **Audit log**: every dispatch logged to Langfuse with full body + target consent provenance + source-URL.
9. **Public-figure threshold**: only addresses listed in faculty directory, journal masthead, festival programmer page, or publicly-linked artist contact. No DM-as-cold-contact.
10. **Constitutional refusal**: any path requiring operator to send is hard-rejected at compile time.

## Three "fresh" mechanics (infrastructure-as-argument)

1. **DataCite Commons RelatedIdentifier graph as discovery surface**. Hapax mints DOIs with `Cites`/`IsSupplementTo`/`Reviews` relations to target ORCIDs. Researchers using DataCite Commons see incoming citations on their dashboard automatically. The *graph* is the message.
2. **ORCID-mediated touch via Zenodo Community publication**. Drop a Hapax artifact into a Zenodo community the target curates. Push-via-curation: not soliciting; contributing to a corpus the target already manages.
3. **Self-federating ActivityPub/RSS surface that targets opt-in to**. Expose a structured ActivityPub actor for each governance file (Refusal Brief, Cohort Disparity). When a target follows the actor, they receive every future revision — Hapax never pushes; the target subscribed.
